Research Overview

This study examines coastal vulnerability and morphological change at Port Fairy Bay, Victoria, Australia, using 7 years of high-resolution UAV-derived topographic data. Through analysis of 54 drone surveys spanning 2018-2025, we investigate how wave conditions and coastal structures influence beach evolution patterns.

Methodology

Data Collection

Beach topography was surveyed using UAV photogrammetry at regular intervals from March 2018 to May 2025. The drone surveys captured high-resolution elevation data across the entire Port Fairy Bay coastline.

Analysis Approach

  • Transect Analysis: Shore-perpendicular transects were established to track cross-shore elevation changes
  • Mean Elevation Change (MEC): Calculated as the average elevation difference between consecutive surveys
  • Beachface Cluster Dynamics (BCD): Classification of morphological states (erosional/accretional)
  • Residual BCD (r-BCD): Overall behavioral regime of each transect across all survey periods
  • Wave Analysis: Correlation with offshore wave data from Sofar Spotter buoy

Python Analysis Code

All data processing and analysis was performed using Python with libraries including:

  • pandas and numpy for data manipulation
  • geopandas for spatial analysis
  • matplotlib and seaborn for visualization
  • scipy for statistical analysis
  • arcpy for GIS operations

Publication

Journal Article

Title: Cross-shore vulnerability gradients and rock armour impacts on sandy beach evolution

Authors: Siegmund Nuyts, et al.

Journal: Coastal Engineering (In Review)

Period: 2018-2025 (54 surveys)

Location: Port Fairy Bay, Victoria, Australia (38°22'S, 142°14'E)

Abstract

This study investigates coastal vulnerability patterns and the influence of rock armour structures on sandy beach morphodynamics using seven years of UAV-derived topographic data from Port Fairy Bay, Australia. Analysis of 54 drone surveys reveals strong correlations between wave forcing and beach elevation change, with maximum wave height (r = 0.61) and wave direction showing significant control on erosion/accretion patterns. The research demonstrates elevation-dependent vulnerability gradients and identifies persistent behavioral regimes associated with coastal protection structures.

Research Impact

This work contributes to understanding climate change impacts on sandy coastlines and provides evidence-based insights for coastal management and adaptation strategies in embayed coastal systems.
